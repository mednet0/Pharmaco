Color-coded therapeutic approach for STEMI/NSTEMI management

Golden Hour Principle

Early intervention (<30 mins) improves outcomes significantly

MONA Protocol

Morphine, Oxygen, Nitrates, Aspirin - Initial stabilization

Antiplatelets

Mechanism: Inhibit platelet aggregation
Examples: Aspirin (162-325mg), Clopidogrel (600mg load), Ticagrelor (180mg load)

Considerations: Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) for 12 months

Anticoagulants

Mechanism: Inhibit thrombin generation
Examples: Heparin, Enoxaparin, Bivalirudin

Considerations: Monitor aPTT (1.5-2.5x control)

Thrombolytics

Mechanism: Fibrinolytic agents (PCI alternative)
Examples: Alteplase, Reteplase, Tenecteplase

Considerations: Door-to-needle time <30 mins

Beta Blockers

Mechanism: Reduce myocardial oxygen demand
Examples: Metoprolol, Carvedilol

Considerations: Avoid in cardiogenic shock

ACE Inhibitors

Mechanism: Reduce ventricular remodeling
Examples: Lisinopril, Ramipril

Considerations: Start within 24hrs if stable

Statins

Mechanism: Plaque stabilization
Examples: Atorvastatin (80mg), Rosuvastatin

Considerations: Initiate regardless of LDL

Analgesics

Mechanism: Pain relief & anxiety reduction
Examples: Morphine, Fentanyl

Considerations: Use cautiously in right ventricular MI
